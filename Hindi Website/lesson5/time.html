Time is what helps us stay on schedule, so that we don't miss the bus or that important deadline at work.

While we're on the topic of days, you might also want to know the ordinal numbers in Hindi. For instance, you want to say "Ansh's birthday is on the 13th of August." In Hindi, this would be expressed as "Ansh ki janmadin terahavaan agast ko hai." As you may have noticed, the months in Hindi are very similar to the months in English. For the first ordinal, you might recognize that the pronunciation is a bit non-intuitive. It sounds like "pehela" instead of "pahala." This phenomenon also occurs for similar words like "sister" in English, which translates to "bahan" in Hindi but sounds like "behen." Ask more about this one.

Next, let's go over how to tell the time. A common mistake most learners (including myself) might make is assuming that the English translation for "what time is it?" is "samay kya hai?" Our intuitions lead us astray here, because the literal English translation for the question in Hindi is "how many chimes?" In Hindi, this would be "kitne baje hai?"

Now of course, you're not going to want to look like a deer in headlights when someone asks you this. So, here's the general answer you would use in most cases: "(number) baje hai/hain."
